Understanding No-Fly Zones in Sri Lanka

When flying drones in Sri Lanka, awareness of legally restricted airspace is critical for safety, compliance, and respect for sensitive sites. Sri Lanka’s Civil Aviation Authority (CAASL) defines several categories of “No-Fly” and controlled zones, while DJI’s GEO System offers a manufacturer-level advisory map integrated into its flight software. This post explains Sri Lanka’s restricted areas and shows how to consult DJI’s database to stay informed before every flight.

CAASL’s Restricted Area Categories

CAASL classifies controlled airspace into three main categories, each requiring specific permissions:

  1. Prohibited Areas (VCP)
    • Landing or overflying by any aircraft is permanently forbidden.
    • Examples include:
      • Parliamentary Complex, Colombo (1 NM radius) [1].
      • Sapugaskanda Oil Refinery (1 NM radius) [1].
      • Colombo City core (complex polygon surrounding central districts) [1].
      • Petroleum storage facilities at Orugodawatta and Kolonnawa (0.25 NM radii) [1][1].
  2. Restricted Areas (VCR)
    • Operations require prior approval from Air Traffic Control (ATC) and Sri Lanka Air Force.
    • Key zones include 5 NM circles around SLAF bases:
      • Anuradhapura (VCR11) [1].
      • China Bay/Trincomalee (VCR12) [1].
      • Hingurakgoda/Minneriya (VCR13) [1].
      • Vavuniya (VCR14) [1].
      • Jaffna (VCR15) [1].
    • Cultural and security-sensitive areas also fall into this category:
      • Kandy’s Temple of the Sacred Tooth Relic (1 NM radius) [1].
      • Diyatalawa Army Camp (2 NM radius) [1].
      • Colombo City Restricted Area excluding pure “Prohibited” polygons (complex boundary) [1].
  3. Danger Areas (VCD)
    • Activated by NOTAMs, typically for naval gunnery exercises.
    • Examples:
      • Naval firing ranges off southwestern and eastern coasts (multiple polygons) [1]–[1].

All controlled-airspace limits are defined by coordinates and altitude, typically from ground level (GND) up to various flight levels (e.g., FL150 or higher) [1].

Key Aerodromes as No-Fly Zones

In addition to VCP/VCR/VCD areas, major aerodromes are effectively no-fly zones for unauthorized UAVs within their lateral limits without explicit ATC clearance:

  • Bandaranaike International Airport, Katunayake (VCBI) [1].
  • Ratmalana (VCCC), Mattala Rajapaksa International (VCRI), Koggala (VCCK), Sigiriya (VCCS), and other certified aerodromes [1].

Checking CAASL’s Geo-Zone Maps

CAASL publishes geospatial zone definitions in PDF and digital formats:

  • Download the “Aerodromes, Prohibited, Restricted and Danger Areas” PDF for detailed coordinates and maps [1].
  • Refer to the CAASL drone portal for updates on temporary restrictions and application procedures: https://www.caa.lk/en/drones.

Always verify the latest NOTAMs and CAASL directives before planning flights near any controlled airspace.

Consulting DJI’s Fly Safe GEO System

DJI’s Geospatial Environment Online (GEO) provides an advisory no-fly map integrated into DJI’s apps and aircraft firmware [2]:

  1. Access the GEO Map
    • Visit https://www.dji.com/flysafe/geo-map and select “Sri Lanka” from the region list [2].
  2. Understand Zone Types
    • Warning & Enhanced Warning Zones: Advisory only; no automatic flight locks.
    • Authorization Zones: Require online self-unlock via a DJI account when authorized by local regulators.
    • Restricted Zones: Hard-locked; cannot be unlocked for national-security sites.
  3. Unlocking Authorized Zones
    • For Authorization Zones, log in with a verified DJI account in DJI GO 4 or DJI Fly app.
    • Submit proof of local flight permission; the app will then permit takeoff within those areas [3].
  4. In-App GEO Warnings
    • During pre-flight checks, DJI apps overlay GEO zone boundaries on the map view and trigger color-coded alerts.
    • Always cross-check DJI advisories with CAASL’s official data—DJI GEO is advisory and may not reflect the latest regulatory changes [2].

Best Practices for Compliance

To ensure lawful, safe operations:

  • Cross-Reference Sources: Always compare CAASL maps, NOTAMs, and DJI GEO data before flying.
  • Obtain Permits Early: Apply for CAASL and, if necessary, local security clearances at least 10 working days in advance.
  • Respect Altitude Limits: Adhere to maximum heights specified both by CAASL (e.g., 400 ft AGL near aerodromes) and DJI GEO.
  • Maintain VLOS: Keep your drone within visual line of sight and away from crowds.
  • Stay Informed: Regulations and geofence boundaries can change—review them before every flight.

By understanding Sri Lanka’s no-fly zones and leveraging both CAASL’s official geo-zone maps and DJI’s Fly Safe GEO system, drone pilots can confidently plan missions that honor regulatory requirements and protect sensitive areas.
