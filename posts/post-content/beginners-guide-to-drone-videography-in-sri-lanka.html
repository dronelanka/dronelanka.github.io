Beginner’s Guide to Drone Videography in Sri Lanka

Sri Lanka’s diverse landscapes—from misty highlands to golden beaches—offer endless opportunities for stunning aerial footage. This guide provides beginner‐friendly camera settings, essential regulatory requirements, and practical tips for safe, compliant, and creative drone videography across the island.

1. Essential Camera Settings for Cinematic Footage

  • Resolution & Frame Rate: Shoot in at least 4K at 24–30 fps for cinematic quality. Higher frame rates (60 fps) enable smooth slow-motion effects in post‐production1.
  • Exposure Triangle:
    • ISO: Keep as low as possible (ISO 100–200) to minimize noise; increase only in low light but test your drone’s noise floor to avoid graininess12.
    • Shutter Speed: Follow the 180° rule—set shutter speed to roughly double your frame rate (e.g., 1/60 s for 30 fps) to achieve natural motion blur. Use ND filters outdoors to maintain this shutter speed in bright conditions2.
  • White Balance: Manually dial the Kelvin temperature to match lighting—≈5 500 K for daylight, 6 500 K for overcast, or lock Auto WB then switch to Manual to freeze the correct setting3.
  • Color Profile & Filters: If available, shoot in a flat or Log profile (e.g., D-Log) for greater grading flexibility. Use ND filters to control exposure without sacrificing shutter speed.

2. Understanding Sri Lankan Drone Regulations

Registration & Permits

All camera-equipped drones (Categories A–C; >200 g) must be registered with the Civil Aviation Authority of Sri Lanka (CAASL), while Category D drones (≤200 g) without cameras are exempt4. Registration is valid for two years and operators must carry their certificate during flights56.

Foreign visitors no longer require Defence Ministry security clearance; they may register and apply for flight approval through CAASL’s online portal (fee ≈ US $50) by specifying locations and time windows at least 10 working days in advance7.

Insurance

Third-party liability insurance is mandatory for Categories A–C. Minimum cover ranges from LKR 100 000 (0.25–0.9 kg) up to LKR 2 500 000+ per kilogram for heavier drones4.

Altitude & Line‐Of‐Sight

Maximum altitude is 100 ft (≈30 m) above ground level and operators must maintain visual line of sight (VLOS) at all times. Flying from moving vehicles or elevated platforms (e.g., rooftops) is prohibited8.

3. Restricted & No-Fly Zones

  • Airports & Military Bases: No flights within a 1 NM (~1.85 km) radius of runways and air bases, including Bandaranaike International Airport9.
  • National Parks & Wildlife Sanctuaries: All UAV operations are banned to protect wildlife from disturbance10.
  • Temples & Heritage Sites: Religious and archaeological sites (e.g., Temple of the Sacred Tooth Relic, Sigiriya) are off-limits without explicit permits from CAASL and the Department of Archaeology11.
  • Public Events & Crowds: Flying over crowds, processions, or urban areas requires special approvals; private operators should avoid large gatherings.

4. Practical Tips for Beginners

  1. Pre‐Flight Planning:
    • Check weather forecasts—avoid gusty winds and rain.
    • Scout safe take-off/landing zones away from people and obstacles.
    • File your flight plan in the CAASL portal, specifying GPS coordinates.
  2. Battery & Equipment Management:
    • Carry fully charged batteries and spares; cold highland mornings reduce flight time.
    • Inspect propellers, gimbal, and firmware updates before each flight.
  3. Flight Techniques:
    • Start with simple, slow movements—straight-forward reveals, gentle orbits, and lateral tracks produce smooth cinematic shots.
    • Use grid lines and the rule of thirds to frame subjects.
    • Vary perspectives: straight-down for abstract patterns (tea terraces, reef formations) and oblique angles to contextualize landscapes.
  4. Safety & Etiquette:
    • Always respect privacy—avoid filming private properties without consent.
    • Maintain at least 50 m distance from people and buildings.
    • Use a spotter to monitor the drone when operating near obstacles or in complex environments.
  5. Post‐Processing:
    • Apply LUTs or color-grade Log footage for rich, balanced images.
    • Stabilize clips if needed, but aim for smooth in-camera movements to reduce reliance on software correction.

By mastering these foundational camera settings, adhering to Sri Lanka’s regulatory framework, and practicing safe, respectful flying techniques, beginners can unlock the island’s breathtaking vistas and cultural treasures through the magic of drone videography.
